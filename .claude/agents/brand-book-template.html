Part 2.3 · Rules

Writing Guidelines

Sentence length

Vary. Mix short punches with longer sentences. Average ~18 words. Never more than 30.

Voice

Active, not passive. “We shipped it.” Not “It was shipped by us.”

Punctuation

Oxford comma: pick one and hold the line. Em dashes: use, sparingly. Exclamation marks: never in headlines.

Capitalization

Sentence case is modern; title case is traditional. Pick one.

Numbers & dates

Numerals for 10+; spell out one through nine.

Hashtags

Branded + 2–4 community/industry tags. Never more than 5.

CTA Formulas

Discovery

See how [specific outcome] →

Commit

Start [specific action] today

Social proof

Join [N] [noun] already [verb]

Urgency

[Action] before [date/limit]

Low-risk

Try [product] free for [N] days

Part 3.1 · Visual

Logo System

Clear space

Minimum clear space equals the height of the lowercase “o” (or equivalent primary character). Nothing may enter this zone.

Minimum size

Print: 10mm wide for the primary lockup. Digital: 80px. Favicon: use the icon-only variant at 32px+.

Responsive behavior

Full lockup on desktop. Wordmark on tablet. Icon only at favicon scale. Never stretch or crop to fit.

Co-branding

Both marks on the same optical baseline, separated by a vertical rule at 50% opacity, with clear space equal to the taller mark’s x-height on either side.

Logo Don’ts

Don’t stretch
Don’t recolor
Don’t rotate
Don’t add effects
Don’t outline
Don’t place on busy photos
Don’t alter spacing
Don’t rearrange elements

Part 3.5 · Layout

Layout & Grid

Grid

8pt base. 12-col desktop, 8-col tablet, 4-col mobile. Gutter: 24px / 16px.

Spacing

Tokens: 4, 8, 12, 16, 24, 32, 48, 64, 96, 128. No arbitrary values.

Content width

Max 1200px container. Body capped at 68ch, display headlines at 42ch.

Cards

4px corner radius (or 0 — pick one). 1px rule at 12% ink. Shadows used sparingly.

Whitespace

Whitespace is a material. If a layout feels sparse, trust it.

Asymmetry

Favor asymmetric over centered, except in ceremonial moments (cover, dividers).

Part 4.1 · Digital

Website & App

Buttons

Primary: filled accent. Secondary: outlined. Tertiary: text + hover underline. Height 44px desktop, 48px mobile.

Inputs

1px rule, 4px radius. Floating labels only in dense forms. Error: accent color + plain-language message.

Cards

Padding 24px. Rule at 12% ink. Hover: 2px lift, no shadow flourish.

Transitions

150–250ms for UI; 400–600ms for page reveal. Easing: cubic-bezier(0.2,0,0,1).

Animation

Reveal-on-scroll for editorial moments only. Motion is a reward, not a tax.

Dark mode

Invert ink/paper. Reduce primary saturation ~10%. Keep accent at full intensity.

Favicon

Icon-only variant. Ship SVG + 32/16px PNG + Apple Touch Icon 180px + theme-color meta tag.

OG share card

1200×630. Logo top-left, headline in display font, brand color background. One template, many headlines.

Part 4.3 · Email

Email & Newsletter

Header / footer

Wordmark top-left. Footer: physical address, unsubscribe, preferences. No social icon forest — 3 max.

Subject line

Under 50 chars. Specific over clever. Never clickbait. At most one emoji, only if it’s the idea.

Preview text

Extend the subject, don’t repeat it. 80–120 characters.

CTA

One primary CTA per email. Secondary as text link. Button: accent color, 44px tall.

Image ratio

Max 40% image, 60% text. Every email readable with images off.

Mobile

Single-column, 600px max. 16px minimum body. Tap targets ≥ 44×44.

Part 5.1 · Print

Print Collateral

Business card

85×55mm, 3mm bleed. Uncoated 350gsm. Front: wordmark. Back: name, role, contact in body font at 8pt.

Letterhead

A4 / US Letter. Wordmark top-left at 24mm wide. Margins: 25/20/30/25mm.

Deck template

16:9. Cover, divider, content, closing slides. Display font for titles only.

One-pager

Headline, 3–5 proof points, CTA. Never more than one page. If it won’t fit, cut.

Invoice / receipt

Wordmark, clear line items, total in accent color. No corporate disclaimers in the customer’s face.

Finishing

Uncoated for warmth; matte laminate only where durability matters. Foil/emboss: ceremonial use only.

Paper stock

FSC-certified. Uncoated 300–350gsm for cards, 100–120gsm for letterhead. Prefer recycled where fidelity allows.

Bleed & marks

3mm bleed on all outer edges. Crop and registration marks on press-ready PDFs.

Packaging

Part 5.3 · Environmental

Signage & Environmental

Exterior signage

Wordmark-only. Halo-lit or backlit only; no external spotlights on the face.

Wayfinding

Body font at all sizes. Iconography in brand icon style. Directional arrows from the icon system only.

Event / booth

Large type, short copy. One CTA per surface. Brand color dominant; accent only on the CTA.

Part 5.4 · Merchandise

Merchandise & Swag

Apparel placement

Chest-left (primary). Center-back (secondary). Sleeve (tertiary, wordmark only). Never full-front unless the item IS the brand.

Embroidery vs. print

Embroidery on heavyweight (jackets, caps). Screen print on lightweight (tees). Never DTG for the wordmark at small sizes.

Approved items

Gift packaging

Uncoated mailer + tissue in brand color + handwritten note. No plastic. No filler.

Part 6.1 · Governance

Brand Dos & Don’ts

Always

  • Use approved logo files from the asset library.
  • Pair display and body fonts as specified.
  • Follow the 60/30/10 color ratio.
  • Check contrast before shipping any text-on-color layout.
  • Write in active voice with one clear CTA per surface.
  • Flag anything ambiguous to the brand owner before publishing.

Never

  • Recreate the logo from scratch in any tool.
  • Use off-brand fonts because a template came with them.
  • Place the logo on busy imagery without clear-space treatment.
  • Use banned vocabulary or competitor tone.
  • Add shadows, gradients, or 3D effects to the logo.
  • Ship to a customer-facing surface without owner sign-off.

Part 6.3 · Workflow

Approval Workflow

Who approves what

  • Marketing copy & social posts → Marketing lead
  • Product UI & in-app copy → Product lead + Brand owner
  • Press, partnerships, external decks → Founder / CEO
  • Legal, regulatory, policy → Legal + Founder
  • Any net-new visual pattern → Brand owner (required)

Review checklist

  • Approved logo file at the right size?
  • Colors on-palette with passing contrast?
  • Fonts from the approved stack?
  • Copy reads in our voice (check We Say / We Don’t)?
  • Exactly one clear CTA?
  • Needs legal review? If unsure, ask.

Part 6.4 · Evolution

Brand Evolution

Proposing changes

One-page proposal: what to change, why, what problem it solves, cost to update assets. Decisions made monthly.

Flex zones

Campaign accents, photo crop/treatment variants, seasonal illustration extensions, typographic display experiments.

Sacred elements

Logo construction. Primary color. Body typeface. Core voice. These don’t change without a full refresh.

Refresh cadence

Minor refinements annually. Full system review every 3 years. Full refresh only when the business has materially changed.

Colophon
